Reibekuchen

Home

Description

Reibekuchen, also called Kartoffelpuffer, are German potato pancakes that are a denser and slightly sweeter version of the Jewish potato latkes. They are a popular street food and are typically served with applesauce or sour cream.

Ingredients

  • 2 potatoes
  • 1 yellow onion
  • 2 eggs
  • 1tsp salt
  • 1/2tsp black pepper
  • 1/2tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1/4c vegetable oil, or more as needed
  • applesauce, sour cream, or another savory sauce for serving

Steps

  1. Peel and finely grate potatoes and onion.
  2. Remove any excess moisture with a fine mesh strainer or a nut milk bag. Catch the liquid in a bowl and let it sit without moving it too much so the potato starch can separate from the water.
  3. Place drained potatoes and onion in another bowl. Add eggs, salt, pepper, and ground nutmeg and stir until combined.
  4. Gently pour leftover water into a small bowl to separate it from the potato starch (the thick white mass sitting at the bottom). Stir potato starch into dough.
  5. Prepare oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add dough in small amounts and shape into pancakes one at a time until the surface of the pan is covered.
  6. Fry for 7-10 minutes per side. Remove from pan and place on a plate with paper towel to absorb excess oil. Add more oil if needed and repeat until there is no dough left.
  7. Serve with sauce of choice.
